Summarized Comments For Revision

* Stanza 1, line 3 needed work: 'these' was ambiguous to many readers and 'bright' received mixed reviews for its use with darker ideas. Changing the line was suggested, which in turn could change line 4 as well.

* Stanza 3, line 1 was too different from the rest of the poem and broke rhythm; changing the format for it was suggested, including using a different choice of words.

* Some suggested changing Stanza 3, line 4 since it seemed out of place. In my opinion, I just don't think they are understanding the image of writing on paper for homework in this stanza.

As It All Fades - First Draft

The cry of a child in the night,
The torment of an empty sleep.
There was a time when these were bright,
But not the flicker, as reasons creep.

Every day a brand new shade,
Upon the old repeated places,
And from the things he had once made,
His life, devotion, leaves only traces.

Trees on trees, day by day,
Hollow shells of thoughts, emotions.
Mechanical hands that swing and sway,
Numerical promotion for human demotion.

The tightened grip on fragile glass,
Liquid takes its shape, and then takes his,
The rest of the world could kiss his ass,
If only he could say it, that is.

His cry, like a child, in the night,
He's tormented by an empty sleep.
These are the things he pulls in tight,
These are more things he struggles to keep.

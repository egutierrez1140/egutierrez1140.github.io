Aesthetic Statement - What I Overheard On My Way Home

This was another piece that touched upon my struggles during my last semester. This time I decided to explore the use of dialogue in a more expressive poetry format. I wanted to make something that had important physical significance. In this case, I decided to use physical space to indicate different speakers on opposite spectrums. By the end of the piece, I believe that it is apparent that this individual was listening to the voices in his head. I based this conversation on an experience that I had when I decided to really listen to what was going on in my head as I drove home after work one night. I found it interesting to listen to myself for a change, and I decided to add some hyperbolic personification to create an interesting scenario. I am particularly proud of what I was able to accomplish with this poem as far as style is concerned, and this piece sparked more ideas for me to experiment with visual presentation of poems.

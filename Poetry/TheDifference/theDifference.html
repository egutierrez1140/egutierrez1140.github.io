Aesthetic Statement - The Difference

Deep down inside, I believe myself to be a simple man. I say this because I tend to find enjoyment in seemingly simple, everyday things. One of those such things is a starry night. I've always found space fascinating; as a kid, I wanted to be an astronaut. Just the idea that we never see the universe as it truly is in the present amazes me on a near-spiritual level. Beyond the great mysteries of the universe, however, seeing a clear night sky is truly a sight to behold, especially since pollution does a great number to how well we can see the stars in cities. This poem is a praise to this beauty in the night. I intentionally try to derail the reader into thinking that I am not literally talking about stars by comparing the sky to a stage, only reveal at the end that I am just, indeed, talking about stars in the sky. I was fun to experiment with that idea, and it actually started out as an accident as I was first writing this poem.

Also, being quite the night owl, I intentionally made the Sun out to be some kind of asshole. I mean, it's just a star, just like all of the billions upon billions of stars that are out there; it gets too much of a special treatment, I think.

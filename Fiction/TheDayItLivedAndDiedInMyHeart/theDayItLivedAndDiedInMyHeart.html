English++

Aesthetic Statement - The Day It Lived and Died In My Heart

I have had a problem with English in my classes since high school. My issues with it have always stemmed from my inability to agree with many of the subjective points that are presented to me and my classmates regarding meanings and grandeur of specific pieces. More often than not, I would have vastly different interpretations from the teacher or the rest of the class, and works deemed to be classics or marvels of their craft would often not sit well with me. The way I saw it (and perhaps still see), instructors were attempting to place definitive, objective labels on topics that, in my opinion, were incapable of concrete labels since words and phrases can have various interpretations if the author does not make their intentions clear. Continued attempts to place objective labels on subjective matters regarding fictions, poetry, etc., lead to me developing a severe hatred for the liberal arts in general, but only within the confines of educational institutions. I wrote this piece as an expression of how I feel about these topics, and as a reminder for the times when I truly enjoyed writing outside of a professional or educational setting. It also serves as a way to briefly express my feelings about making works too complicated for people to understand them.

Aesthetic Statement - An Unusual Scar

This was the very first piece that I pursued during the course. In it, I recount a very specific event that took place in my childhood while my family was living in Chicago. The event in question was a nightmare, one of the very few that I have had in my entire life. To this day, I can still clearly see the vivid images that I first experienced all those years ago. Despite the somewhat horrific nature of the nightmare itself, this memory is one that I think of quite fondly, due to the the events that I decided to correlate with it. I do not really want to ruin the twist, so I think I'll leave you hanging here.

As far as progress with this piece, I had a very rough start. I have never been a "writer"; I do not have the heart for it. During the first few weeks of the course, I struggled with being comfortable with writing anything, out of fear that it simply wasn't good enough. Looking back, that still holds true, but being able to accept that I won't be writing something amazing all of the time really helped me grow throughout the semester. I'm pretty sure that this piece is more like a flash fiction than anything else, but because it's essentially a re- telling of something from my own past and I approached it as such, I shall keep it here as a creative non-fiction work.
